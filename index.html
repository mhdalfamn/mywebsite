Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug was born in 1894 in Iowa, United States. He studied agriculture at Iowa State University and went on to pursue his doctorate at the University of Minnesota.

During his career, Dr. Borlaug developed numerous wheat varieties that were resistant to pests and diseases. These varieties significantly increased wheat yields in many parts of the world, particularly in Asia and Africa.

For his contributions to agriculture, Dr. Borlaug was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1970. He is considered one of the most influential scientists of the 20th century.
